What if I'm in crisis right now? +
🆘 Please reach out immediately. Call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) — available 24/7, free, and confidential. You can also text HOME to 741741 (Crisis Text Line). You matter, and help is available right now.
Is this website completely free? +
Yes — completely and always free. Every article, tool, journaling prompt and resource on this site is available to anyone without signing up or paying anything. Our mission is access for everyone.
Is this a replacement for therapy? +
No — and we're honest about that. Healthy Minds Journal is an educational and supportive resource, not a clinical service. Our content can complement therapy beautifully, but it is not a substitute for professional mental health treatment. If you think you may benefit from therapy, we encourage you to explore our therapist directory.
Who writes the articles? +
Our articles are written by mental health advocates, writers with lived experience, and reviewed by licensed therapists and counsellors. We combine personal humanity with clinical accuracy so that every piece is both trustworthy and genuinely relatable.
Can I stay anonymous? +
Absolutely. You never need to create an account or share any personal information to read our content. Even our newsletter only requires your email address — nothing else. Your privacy is deeply important to us.
How often is new content published? +
We publish new articles every week. If you subscribe to our newsletter, you'll receive a curated weekly email with our latest articles, a daily affirmation, and one practical wellness tool — delivered gently to your inbox every Sunday.
Can I write for Healthy Minds Journal? +
Yes! We welcome submissions from mental health advocates, people with lived experience, and professionals. We believe in the power of diverse voices. Visit our Write for Us page to learn about our guidelines and submit a pitch.
